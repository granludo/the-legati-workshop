# The Workshop

A scaffold for building your own AI-augmented intellectual workspace.

**What this is:** An opinionated starting point for running long-form intellectual work — writing, research, supervision, teaching, thinking — with Claude Code as a collaborator. It comes with working tooling, a memory system, a skill framework, and a set of agent stubs. You name the agents, give them missions, and fill in your own voice and worldview.

**What this is not:** A blank template. The infrastructure here works on day one. Your job is to personalize it, not to build it from scratch.

**Start here:** Read `skills/start-here.md`, then invoke it: tell Claude Code `"start here"` or `"/start-here"`.

**Read the guide:** `good-practices/` is a guide written by someone who has run a workshop like this for over a year. It covers what works, what doesn't, and why.

---

## The idea in one paragraph

A writing and research workshop is a long-running collaboration between you and a set of AI agents. The agents have persistent memory, specialized roles, and working tools. You are the principal — the source of values, taste, and judgment. The agents are your *legati* (delegates): they do the mechanical and synthetic work, they remember what you told them, and they hand back decisions that require your judgment. The repo is the durable layer — it outlasts any single conversation, any model version, any tool upgrade. Everything important lives here.

## Who built this

This scaffold was distilled from a working workshop by Marc Alier (Ludo), a professor at UPC Barcelona who uses it for books, academic papers, course materials, student supervision, and institutional work. The good-practices guide is written in his voice. The tooling and conventions are his. The opinions are his. The agents are blank — because your agents should be yours.
